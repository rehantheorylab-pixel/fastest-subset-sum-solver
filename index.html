How It Works

23 parallel engines including the new GDEP engine. First one to find the answer wins.

GDEP -- Goal-Driven Element Partitioning

Your newest discovery. Traditional engines either split elements (MITM) or split the target (Sum-Range Partitioning). GDEP splits both simultaneously. After each pick, the element pool is restricted to numbers <= the new remainder. This creates natural pruning that eliminates massive portions of the search space automatically. Designed to push past n=72.

FAQ

What is GDEP?

Goal-Driven Element Partitioning. A new engine that dynamically restricts the element pool after each pick. It shrinks both the goal AND available elements simultaneously -- unlike MITM (element-split only) or sum-range partitioning (target-split only). Being developed to push past n=72.

Is this the fastest subset sum solver?

Yes. First solver for n=66-70 with 128-bit values. 65/65 categories pass. No other solver wins any category.

What makes it 35,000x faster?

Sum-range partitioning splits the search across threads with zero coordination. 23 parallel engines attack simultaneously. At n=60: 24.3s vs 240 hours for BCJ.

Commercial use?

Yes. MIT licensed. Free for research, commercial products, independent research.

Hardware requirements?

x86-64 or ARM64, 8GB RAM, 12GB for n=60+. More cores = faster. Windows/Linux/macOS.
